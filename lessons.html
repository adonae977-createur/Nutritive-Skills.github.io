Nutrition Basics

Learn the fundamental building blocks of nutrition and why they're important for your body.

What are Nutrients?

Nutrients are substances in food that provide energy and help your body function. There are six main types:

  • Carbohydrates: Main source of energy
  • Proteins: Build and repair body tissues
  • Fats: Store energy and support cell function
  • Vitamins: Support immune system and metabolism
  • Minerals: Essential for bone health and fluid balance
  • Water: Vital for all bodily functions

Balanced Plate

Discover how to create meals that provide all the nutrients your body needs.

The Plate Method

A balanced meal should include:

  • ½ plate: Non-starchy vegetables (broccoli, spinach, carrots)
  • ¼ plate: Lean protein (chicken, fish, beans, tofu)
  • ¼ plate: Whole grains or starchy vegetables (brown rice, sweet potatoes)
  • A serving of fruit and dairy (or alternatives)

This method ensures you get a variety of nutrients and helps control portion sizes.

Reading Food Labels

Learn to decode nutrition labels and make informed food choices.

Key Parts of a Nutrition Label

  • Serving Size: The amount of food in one serving
  • Calories: Energy from one serving
  • Nutrients: Amounts of fats, carbs, proteins, vitamins
  • % Daily Value: How much of your daily needs this provides
  • Ingredients List: What's in the food (in order of quantity)

Look for foods with low added sugars, sodium, and saturated fats.

Importance of Hydration

Understand why water is crucial for health and how to stay properly hydrated.

Why Hydration Matters

Water is essential for:

  • Regulating body temperature
  • Transporting nutrients and oxygen
  • Removing waste products
  • Lubricating joints
  • Digesting food

Signs of dehydration: dry mouth, fatigue, dizziness, dark urine.

Aim for 8-10 glasses (2-3 liters) of water daily, more if you're active.

Healthy Snacking

Learn how to choose nutritious snacks that keep you energized between meals.

Smart Snack Choices

Healthy snacks combine protein, fiber, and healthy fats:

  • Apple slices with peanut butter
  • Greek yogurt with berries
  • Carrot sticks with hummus
  • Handful of nuts and dried fruit
  • Whole grain crackers with cheese

Avoid snacks high in added sugars, salt, or unhealthy fats.

Nutrition and Exercise

Discover how nutrition supports physical activity and recovery.

Fueling Your Body

Before exercise: Eat carbs for energy, some protein for muscle support.

During exercise: Stay hydrated, consume electrolytes if exercising >90 minutes.

After exercise: Eat protein and carbs within 30-60 minutes for recovery.

Daily nutrition: Ensure adequate calories, protein, and micronutrients for active lifestyle.
